Stable or Bull Market Regime:
Characterized by low volatility and steady positive returns.
Markets tend to reward risk-taking with limited drawdowns.

Uncertain or Transition Regime:
Characterized by rising volatility and mixed returns.
Markets lack clear direction and risk of drawdowns increases.

Crisis or High Volatility Regime:
Characterized by extreme volatility and large drawdowns.
These regimes are usually short-lived but account for most losses.
